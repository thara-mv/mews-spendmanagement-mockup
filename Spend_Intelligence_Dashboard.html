Honest Limitations

What "real-time" actually means
Ledger is not real-time
NetSuite postings are batched. GL actuals lag T+1 at best, T+5 if accruals are involved. Full close happens monthly.
Payroll lag is structural
Payroll finalises mid-month after the prior period's run. We model expected payroll from HRIS headcount × rate cards, then true-up to actual at close.
GL ≠ Budget category
Chart of accounts and budget structure rarely map 1:1. A mapping table maintained by FP&A is required, and it drifts as the business evolves.
Accruals require judgement
"Real burn" = paid + invoiced + accrued. Accruals are estimates. Anomaly thresholds need to allow for accrual noise near month-end.
Approvals ≠ posted transactions
An approved request isn't in the books until invoice arrives. Until then it's a commitment — visible in the model, not in the ledger.

Access Control

Who sees what, and why
CFO / VP Strategic Finance
Full visibility across all categories. Consolidated view, anomaly feed across the company, all approval requests.
Role: admin
Budget Owner (CTO, CMO, CCO, CPO)
Sees only the categories they own. Cannot see peer categories. Anomaly feed scoped to their cost centres only.
Role: owner
Finance Business Partner
Inherits the scope of the leader they partner with. Can route approvals on their behalf.
Role: fbp
Requester (any IC)
Can submit a spend request. Sees only their own submissions and their status. No access to budget data.
Role: requester
Audit log
Every view, approval, and override logged. Quarterly access review.
